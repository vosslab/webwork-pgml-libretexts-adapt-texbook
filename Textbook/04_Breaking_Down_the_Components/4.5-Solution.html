Solution

The Solution is for trust and alignment, not grading. It shows students what the system expected and helps instructors confirm that the checker matches the stated method.

The solution must use the same conventions the checker uses. If the checker expects a numeric value in μM to two decimals, the solution should show 0.50 μM, not 0.5 mM.

Why this exists: it clarifies the target, the method, and the reporting format that the grader expects.

Common edits Common failures
  • Add a short formula line and unit reminder.
  • Note the rounding rule used by the checker.
  • Solution implies a different tolerance.
  • Units shown do not match the blank.

Solution checklist

Include Why One-sentence example
Restate givens Confirms the student is using the same data. "Given c_stock and V_stock in μM and μL..."
Show the formula Keeps the method transparent. "Use c_final = c_stock * V_stock / V_final."
Show substitution Connects values to the computed target. "Substitute 10 * 2 / 40."
Final units/format Matches what the checker expects. "Report in μM to two decimals."
Rounding rule Prevents mismatch with tolerance. "Use the same rounding as the answer checker."
