This quickstart is designed to give you a complete win early: take a working PGML-first problem, copy it, and make a small edit that still grades correctly.

Quickstart steps

  1. Copy a working template problem (start from Chapter 4 if you have one available).
  2. Change only the story text first, keeping the variable names and math intact.
  3. Preview multiple variants to confirm the prompt still makes sense.
  4. Then change the numbers or the formula, one small change at a time.

Do not start by changing variable names or answer objects; change text first.

A minimal PGML-first skeleton (for copy-editing)

The file below is intentionally complete and minimal: it includes macros, setup, a PGML prompt, and an answer blank tied directly to its answer object. 

DOCUMENT();

loadMacros(
	"PGstandard.pl",
	"MathObjects.pl",
	"PGML.pl",
	"PGcourse.pl",
);

Context("Numeric");

$a = random(2, 8, 1);
$b = random(3, 9, 1);
$ans = Real($a + $b);

BEGIN_PGML
In a lab notebook, two counts were recorded: [$a] and [$b].

What is the total count?

[`Total = `] [________]{$ans}
END_PGML

ENDDOCUMENT();

After you can copy-edit a problem like this without breaking grading, you are ready to expand into tables, multi-part prompts, and tighter answer checking.

Apply it today

  • Copy a working file, change one small thing, and preview multiple variants before changing anything else.
  • Keep student-facing sentences in PGML and keep regular PG focused on values and answer checking.
  • After copy-editing, preview in ADAPT, and if ADAPT reports only a generic error, use Chapter 7 (webwork-pg-renderer) to get a line-level error and fix it fast.
