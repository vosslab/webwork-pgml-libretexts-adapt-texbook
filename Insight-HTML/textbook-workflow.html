What is the workflow for building a LibreTexts Remix or Textbook?

Source: https://commons.libretexts.org/insight/textbook-workflow
Last updated at 09/05/2025 09:59 PM by Delmar Larsen

Description: The general workflow for building a LibreTexts Remix or Textbook can be adapted depending on whether you’re creating a small curated remix, a course reader, or a full-scale textbook.

Here’s a clear workflow for building a LibreTexts Remix or Textbook. The process can be adapted depending on whether you’re creating a small curated remix, a course reader, or a full-scale textbook.

  1. Get a LibreOne account at https://register.libretexts.org
  2. Create a Conductor Project for your remix (at https://conductor.libretexts.org). This is the project management tool for the LibreVerse.
  3. Create a New book in the Conductor project (there is a button for this on your Conductor page)
  4. Build a Remixing Map   
  5. Open the Remixer (there is a button) to remix from this book.
  6. Edit your book as you see fit (there is an edit button on the remix pages although you will need to "fork" the remixed pages to edit in most cases)
  7. Cycle through 4 and 5 until you are happy to publish
  8. Request the book to be published (there is a button on the Conductor page for this).
  9. We then run some checks, move your book to the bookshelf, and compile it for export options.
  10. Use the book as needed.
  11. Maintain and Update the text as needed
