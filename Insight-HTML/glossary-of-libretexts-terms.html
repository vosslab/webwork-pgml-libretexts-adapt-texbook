Glossary of LibreTexts Terms

Source: https://commons.libretexts.org/insight/glossary-of-libretexts-terms
Last updated at 07/07/2024 02:59 AM by Delmar Larsen

Description: A brief glossary of terms within the LibreVerse

  1. ADAPT: LibreTexts' adaptive learning and assessment system
  2. Commons & Conductor: A platform for browsing and managing open education resources (OER)
  3. H5P: A framework for creating interactive assignments and learning tools. Frequently used for creating homework questions in ADAPT
  4. Insight: A ‘knowledge base’ for all things LibreTexts
  5. Instructor Verification: LibreTexts' verification process that, if approved, allows users to create and edit textbooks, assessments, and more. Instructor verification happens at the individual level. Registering for a LibreOne account with a ‘.edu’ address does not automatically grant instructor access
  6. LibreOne: The Central Authentication Service that manages LibreTexts user accounts. With one set of credentials, users can access all of LibreTexts' services and applications (ADAPT support coming ~Summer 2024)
  7. LibreNet: The LibreTexts project’s consortium that provides member campuses/districts with added benefits and access to the greater LibreVerse suite of open technologies. See https://libretexts.org/about-us/our-network/
  8. LibreTexts: LibreTexts is a 501(c)(3) non-profit organization committed to freeing the textbook from the limitations and costs of traditional textbooks. Our open and freely accessible textbooks provide a more engaging learning experience for students without the financial burden
  9. LibreVerse: A colloquial, collective term for the entirety of LibreTexts' services and applications (ADAPT, Commons, Studio, etc.)
  10. Remixer: A tool for combining existing OER textbook pages to create new textbooks
  11. Studio: LibreTexts' repository for H5P questions, interactive videos, and other interactive learning materials
  12. Support Center: LibreTexts' central hub for user support. Users can submit support tickets, read knowledge base articles (see ‘Insight’), and check for known outages. Users can quickly access the Support Center by click on the blue question mark widget in the bottom right corner of any LibreTexts application
  13. WeBWorK: An open-source homework system, primarily used in math and science. Frequently used for creating homework questions in ADAPT
