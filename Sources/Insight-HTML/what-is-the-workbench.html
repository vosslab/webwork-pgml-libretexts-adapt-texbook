What is the Workbench?

Last updated at 07/07/2024 02:48 AM by Delmar Larsen

Description: Workbenches provide an enhanced user experience for your creating and managing your OER on the LibreTexts libraries.

What is Workbench?

Workbench, released December 2023, refers to the latest iteration of OER creation workspaces in the LibreTexts libraries. Previously, contributors would create a "Sandbox" through the library interface directly, and provide a link to it in a Conductor project, if desired.

Workbench enhances this functionality, by instead allowing contributors to create and administer a book in a library directly through Conductor.

New Book Tool

To get started with Workbench and create a new book, create a new Project in Conductor:

After creation, click the "Edit Properties" button at the top of the page:

Within the modal that opens, you will see a "Create Book" button:

In the Create Book modal, you'll select the library that your want your book to reside in, and give it a title. Please use caution when selecting a library, as this can't be changed.

When ready, click the "Create" button. Please be patient, as it may take a few moments for Conductor to setup your book in the library. Note: Book titles must be unique within their library. You'll receive an error notifying you if a book with the same title already exists.

After succesful creation, you should see the "Book Created" label in your Project Properties:

You can click on this label at any time to take you directly to your book in its library!

Managing Team Permissions

When a book is created following the steps above, Conductor will automatically manage its editor permissions for you!

To manage your project's team, click the "Manage Team" button at the top of your project's page:

Here, you can add and remove users to your team as necessary:

Now, any time you add a user to your project's team, they'll automatically be given editor (aka Contributor) to your book! Similarly, when a user is removed from a team, their permissions to your book will also be revoked.

An Afterword:

As of December 2023, Workbench (and its associated tools and features) is still in its initial release phase. Over time, and as more user feedback is received, additional tools and functionality will continue to be added to further Conductor's role as a centralized tool for managing your OER content.
